Sentiment Analysis with Log-Likelihood

  • Log-likelihood dictionary:
    $$ \lambda(w) = \log \frac{P(w \mid pos)}{P(w \mid neg)} $$

  • Logprior:
    $$ \text{logprior} = \log \frac{D_{pos}}{D_{neg}} = 0 $$

  • Tweet:
    $$ [\text{"I"}, \text{"pass"}, \text{"the"}, \text{"NLP"}, \text{"interview"}] $$

  • Calculation of the score: $$ \text{score} = -0.01 + 0.5 - 0.01 + 0 + \text{logprior} = 0.48 $$

  • Prediction:
    $$ \text{pred} = \text{score} > 0 $$

The example above shows how you can make a prediction given your \(\lambda\) dictionary. In this example the \(text{logprior}\) is 0 because we have the same amount of positive and negative documents (i.e. \(\log(1)=0\)).
